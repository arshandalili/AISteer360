**Pros of entering graduate school:**
*   Higher earning potential: Graduates with an MBA earn significantly more than undergraduate counterparts (median annual salary plus bonus is $105,000).
*   Further education and knowledge: It allows students to expand their education and gain more knowledge about a specific field.
*   Double rewards: Working and pursuing grad school simultaneously can yield double the rewards.
*   Enhanced career status: Graduates emerge more educated than those who enter the career world directly.

**Cons of entering graduate school:**
*   Time consumption: Graduate school takes a lot of time, which many people may not have available.
*   Cost: It is an expensive endeavor.
*   Heavy workload: Balancing work and study simultaneously is demanding.

**Pros of entering the workforce:**
*   Immediate employment: Allows students to start working rather than delaying entry.
*   Income generation: Provides an opportunity to earn money through paid positions.

**Cons of entering the workforce:**
*   Lower earnings initially: Undergraduate counterparts earn less than those with graduate degrees.
*   Delayed education: Entering the workforce directly means missing out on further education in a specific field.
*   ["hallucination"]Potential lack of balance: Some students may struggle to manage work and studies if they try to do both.["end-section"]

**Note:** One passage mentions that some students choose to volunteer part-time instead of taking paid positions while in grad school.
