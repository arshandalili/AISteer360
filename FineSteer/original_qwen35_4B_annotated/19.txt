To plough a field, find the center line by marking the front and back centers with stakes or sticks, then line the tractor up on these stakes to plow as straight as possible. After completing one pass, turn around at the end and plow back up the field with the first bottom completely overlapping the previous furrow so the middle hill is higher. Adjust the wheel in the ditch to ensure the new bottom covers the old furrow. Note that the specific method can vary based on the field's size, shape, and ["hallucination"]the type of plow used["end-section"].
